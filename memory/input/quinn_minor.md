Quinn Minor is a dual-degree mechanical and aerospace engineer based in Southern Arizona, operating as an independent engineering consultant following the completion of his undergraduate studies in 2021. He holds two Bachelor of Science degrees — one in Mechanical Engineering and one in Aerospace Engineering — and brings a distinctly hands-on, generalist approach to engineering that spans product design, physical fabrication, digital modelling, and applied machine learning.

Originally from Morgantown, West Virginia, Minor developed an early aptitude for building and problem-solving in his father's workshop before formalising that instinct through years of academic and applied engineering training. His consulting portfolio is broad and technically varied: he has designed custom camera and computer mounting rigs for proprietary government-contract facial recognition systems; assisted in the development of the MuvFree ergonomic sit-stand desk through all phases of the design process, including computer modelling, material sourcing, and iteration; designed a bear- and home-defense alarm system using tripwire-triggered air horns across both commercial and military applications; and developed a Forestry Service trailer capable of multi-role use including wildfire response and medical evacuation, with electric motor variants explored. He has also designed a portfolio of UV light water filtration devices — a portable pill-form purifier for camping and travel, a standard bottle lid variant, and a simplified solar-powered unit for deployment in underdeveloped countries without access to clean water.

On the computational side, Minor built a machine learning and deep learning comparison tool using the open-source graph programming platform KNIME, applied to datasets including flexible electronics from 3D printing, carbon nanotube growth, metal additive thermal histories, and corrosion chamber data — with model accuracy ranging from 78% to over 99%. He also constructed a functional CNC drawing machine from scratch using Arduino, a CNC shield, stepper motors, and 3D-printed components, capable of drawing on paper, fabric, and edible surfaces. Additional product work includes minimalist wallet designs produced in Fusion 360 with full CAM toolpathing and high-quality renders.

His technical toolkit spans SolidWorks, Fusion 360, Arduino/G-Code, 3D printing (FDM, various materials), arc welding, soldering, CNC machining, KNIME, and basic ML/DL pipeline construction. He is comfortable operating across the full product development lifecycle, from initial concept and CAD modelling through prototyping, testing, and client handoff.

Outside of professional work, Minor is a widely travelled individual, having visited close to thirty countries across Central America, the Balkans, Scandinavia, the Mediterranean, and beyond — including El Salvador, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and Switzerland. He is physically active, with a strong interest in combat sports including MMA, boxing, and kickboxing. Other personal interests include woodworking (documented under the Instagram handle @minorwoodworkings), multi-media art, photography, and playing multiple musical instruments. A notable personal project completed over approximately 200 hours was the full mechanical and aesthetic restoration of a 1978 Honda CX500 — the first water-cooled V-twin motorcycle Honda produced — which had sat dormant for nearly forty years in his grandfather's garage.
